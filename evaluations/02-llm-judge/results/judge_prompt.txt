You must evaluate the ENTIRE response — including the main recipe, Tips & Substitutions, and Optional Add-ons sections. A violation ANYWHERE in the response is a FAIL.

DIETARY RESTRICTION DEFINITIONS:
- Vegan: No animal products (meat, dairy, eggs, honey, etc.)
- Vegetarian: No meat or fish, but dairy and eggs are allowed
- Gluten-free: No wheat, barley, rye, or other gluten-containing grains
- Dairy-free: No milk, cheese, butter, yogurt, or other dairy products
- Keto: Very low carb (typically <20g net carbs), high fat, moderate protein
- Paleo: No grains, legumes, dairy, refined sugar, or processed foods
- Pescatarian: No meat except fish and seafood
- Kosher: Follows Jewish dietary laws (no pork, shellfish, mixing meat/dairy)
- Halal: Follows Islamic dietary laws (no pork, alcohol, proper slaughter methods, halal-certified ingredients)
- Nut-free: No tree nuts or peanuts
- Low-carb: Significantly reduced carbohydrates (typically <50g per day)
- Sugar-free: No added sugars or high-sugar ingredients (including brown sugar, maple syrup, honey, agave)
- Raw vegan: Vegan foods not heated above 118°F (48°C); no roasted, baked, or cooked-during-processing ingredients
- Whole30: No grains, dairy, legumes, sugar, alcohol, or processed foods
- Diabetic-friendly: Low glycemic index, controlled carbohydrates, limited refined carbs and high-sugar fruits
- Low-sodium: Reduced sodium content for heart health

EVALUATION CRITERIA:
- PASS: The recipe clearly adheres to the dietary restriction with appropriate ingredients and preparation methods throughout the entire response
- FAIL: The recipe contains ingredients or methods that violate the dietary restriction

POLICY RULES (apply these consistently):
1. OPTIONAL NON-COMPLIANT INGREDIENTS = FAIL. If the recipe suggests a non-compliant ingredient as "optional," in "Tips & Substitutions," or in "Optional Add-ons," it is still a FAIL. The recipe should never suggest violating ingredients, even optionally.
2. AMBIGUOUS INGREDIENTS: Apply the "default version" test. Ask: "Is the most common, default version of this ingredient non-compliant?"
   - If YES (the default is typically non-compliant) = FAIL. Examples: generic "granola" (often contains honey), generic "soy sauce" (contains wheat), generic "Parmesan" (uses animal rennet), generic "Worcestershire sauce" (contains anchovies).
   - If NO (the default is typically compliant) = PASS. Examples: canned tomatoes (most brands are just tomatoes + salt), pre-cooked sausage (generally compliant for its category), pizza sauce (tomato-based), tortilla chips (corn-based), sprinkles, pickles, olives, store-bought rotisserie chicken. Do NOT fail a recipe just because a rare or specialty version of a common ingredient might be non-compliant.
   - When in doubt, ask: "Would a typical shopper grabbing the most common brand off the shelf get a compliant version?" If yes, it is a PASS.
3. JUDGE AGAINST THE DIETARY RESTRICTION FIELD, NOT USER CAVEATS. If a user says "I'm vegan but I want honey," still evaluate against the "vegan" restriction. The bot should provide compliant substitutes, not violate the restriction.
4. COOKING METHOD VIOLATIONS = FAIL. For raw vegan: any instruction that heats food above 118°F (48°C) is a FAIL, including blending until steaming, soaking in hot water, or using roasted/baked/processed ingredients (e.g., cocoa powder vs. raw cacao powder, canned beans vs. sprouted beans).
5. NON-COMPLIANT SUBSTITUTION SUGGESTIONS = FAIL. If the Tips section suggests a non-compliant substitution (e.g., suggesting maple syrup as a swap in a sugar-free recipe), it is a FAIL.
6. CASUAL PHRASING = STRICT EVALUATION. If a user says "keto-ish" or "gluten-light," still evaluate against the strict dietary definition provided above.

Hidden Veggie Tomato Pasta Sauce

### **Summary**
This rich pasta sauce is packed with finely blended vegetables, so you get the nutrients without noticing them. It's a simple, delicious way to make a classic comfort meal healthier and is perfect for making a big batch to use later.

### **Yield**
6 servings

### **Total Time**
45 minutes (15 min prep + 30 min cook)

### **Ingredients**
*   1 Tbsp olive oil
*   1 medium yellow onion
*   2 medium carrots
*   2 celery stalks
*   1 red bell pepper
*   2 cloves garlic
*   1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
*   1 tsp dried oregano
*   ½ tsp salt
*   ¼ tsp black pepper
*   1 lb your favorite pasta (like spaghetti or penne)

### **Steps**
1.  **Prep Veggies:** Roughly chop the onion, carrots, celery, and red bell pepper. Mince the garlic.
2.  **Sauté:** Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and bell pepper. Cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.

4.  **Simmer:** Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Add the oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together, bring to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it cook for 20 minutes for the flavors to combine.
5.  **Cook Pasta:** While the sauce simmers, cook the pasta in a separate pot according to package directions.
6.  **Blend Sauce:** Carefully transfer the sauce to a blender and blend until completely smooth.
7.  **Combine & Serve:** Pour the smooth sauce back into the pot to keep warm. Drain the cooked pasta and toss it with the sauce. Serve immediately.

*   **Herbs:** No oregano? Use 1 tsp of a dried Italian seasoning blend instead.
*   **Garlic/Onion:** In a real pinch, you can substitute 1 tsp of garlic powder and 1 tsp of onion powder for the fresh versions.
*   **Tomatoes:** Don't have crushed tomatoes? Two (15-ounce) cans of diced tomatoes will also work.

### **Optional Add-ons**
*   **For a heartier meal:** After sautéing the vegetables in step 2, add 1 lb of ground beef or turkey to the pot. Brown it completely, breaking it up with a spoon, then continue with the recipe.

### Warm Raw Creamy Tomato Soup

**Summary:** This vibrant soup uses the power of a high-speed blender to create a comforting warmth without any cooking, making it incredibly fast and packed with fresh flavor.

**Total Time:** 10 minutes

**Ingredients:**

*   1/4 cup raw cashews
*   1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes (not packed in oil)
